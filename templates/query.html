Needle and tube thoracostomy

Needle thoracostomy is accomplished by cleansing the skin in the upper chest and inserting the catheter over needle into the second intercostal space (just over the third rib) at the midclavicular line.

Common complications:

cardiac tamponade, life-threatening bleeding due to pulmonary artery or intercostal vessel injury, nontherapeutic (i.e., ineffective) NT insertion, and nerve injury/neuralgia at the insertion site.
